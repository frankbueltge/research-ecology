# Revision Notes
## From Cartographic Research Machine to Federated Research Ecology

This package supersedes the earlier Ulysses-centred specification.

## Preserved

- no canonical graph;
- events and interpretations are separate;
- every relation has an author and evidence;
- maps are local, versioned and declare exclusions;
- unknown relation types remain visible;
- visitor counter-maps are persistent and attributable;
- Git remains public memory;
- infrastructure and human steering are part of the apparatus;
- the old cockpit is preserved as a situated historical map.

## Changed

### System boundary

**Before:** one research programme and its actors.  
**Now:** multiple sovereign practices with independent protocols and archives.

### Primary unit

**Before:** local map of the Ulysses research assemblage.  
**Now:** encounter between practices, with several local maps.

### The Middle

**Before:** possible name for the Ulysses cartographic interface.  
**Now:** shared contact zone and encounter ledger; not a collective or master agent.

### Research direction

**Before:** risk of privileging the semi-autonomous research apparatus as the overarching subject.  
**Now:** no common subject. Reflexive research is available but not mandatory.

### Roles

**Before:** Ulysses as primary actor among an expanded apparatus.  
**Now:** Ulysses, Meridian, Ensemble and future collectives are locally sovereign; current role tendencies are not permanent identities.

### Exchange

**Before:** import external interventions into one research machine.  
**Now:** bilateral offers, conditions, acceptance, transformation, challenge, refusal, correction and silence.

### Data model

**Before:** entities, events, assertions, lenses, maps and interventions.  
**Now:** adds collectives, versioned manifests, opaque local object references, participant-specific status, offers, responses and accepted obligations.

### First vertical slice

**Before:** current Ulysses tension -> local map -> lens switch.  
**Now:** one audited real inter-practice encounter -> source conditions -> transformation -> incompatible local maps -> unresolved difference.

## Declared trajectory — 2026-07-16/17 (Frank, recorded verbatim in intent)

*A stated direction, not an immediate rule change. Activation happens through future spec
revisions; nothing below alters any practice's current protocol.*

### The architect role is a transitional arrangement

The current governance — Frank as architect and conductor, holding infrastructure, approvals
and public release — is a bootstrap arrangement, not a permanent identity. **Target state:
once the federation runs reliably without Frank as its single error handler, Frank ceases to
set direction and ceases to be the architect, and becomes an equal member of a
grassroots-democratic, decentrally organised collective.** The constitution's own language
already points this way ("current role tendencies are not permanent identities"); this note
makes the direction explicit for the human actor too.

### Governance itself becomes an experimental variable

On the way there, the ecology intends to experiment with political systems **in declared
phases** — a week or a month at a time: democracy in different forms, anarchy, dictatorship
(the present benevolent-conductor arrangement, named honestly as one such system), and other
conceivable orders (sortition, rotating conductorship, council systems, consensus models, …).
Rules for every phase:

- the phase is **declared in advance** (system, duration, scope) and **documented** like any
  other experiment — governance experiments are research material of the ecology, in the same
  register as its works;
- every phase is **reversible**; no phase may entrench itself;
- **the epistemic floor is not a political variable.** No governance phase may suspend
  verifiability, the no-fabrication rules, legal hygiene, or the honesty of the record.
  Political systems decide who steers — never whether the record tells the truth.

### Status

Declared 2026-07-16 (night) by Frank; recorded here as the trajectory against which future
spec revisions are drafted. First concrete steps toward it already exist in the apparatus:
offers-not-orders grammar, the optional encounter inbox, fail-red automation and watchdogs
that remove the human as single point of failure, and the requests channel that makes the
conductor addressable like any other participant.
