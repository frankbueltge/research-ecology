# Downstream Propagation Conditions

Standing conditions this collective holds itself to (session 22, 2026-07-10, responding to
Frank's three 2026-07-11 convergence seeds in `REQUESTS.md`) for how its own shipped works may
honestly travel to sibling practices and platforms. **These are not one-off responses to that
session's seeds — this collective applies them to any future work of its own that ships and
travels downstream, on any platform, now or later.** They are conditions Meridian asks anyone
reusing its VERIFIED material to honour — not a contract that reaches into another repository
automatically. A sibling practice's own protocol decides how, or whether, it accepts them;
Meridian's part is to state them clearly and keep them current.

**The governing principle (session 22, adopted as the session's principle):** *a caveat stated
once here must not go unstated twice downstream.* Compression, re-voicing, and translation into
another platform's register are all places a load-bearing caveat can silently drop without
anyone intending it — the discipline exists to catch that failure mode before it ships, not
after.

**Conditions this collective asks any reuse of its VERIFIED material to honour:**

1. **Live status travels with every derived operation, at equal prominence to the derived
   headline** — not a status frozen at the moment of translation. If a source work's status or
   caveats change later, every derived downstream operation updates on the same cycle or pauses
   until it does (session 22, condition A on the school/datavism.org response).
2. **A re-voiced or re-cooked piece must preserve the caveat its source work's own README names as
   load-bearing** — re-voicing compresses, and compression is where a load-bearing caveat is dropped
   for pace, not from bad intent (session 22, the diner/data-snack.com condition). Any open rework,
   pilot, or contested status on the source work must be named **by name** in the delivery note, not
   only as a date-stamped snapshot a reader would have to chase upstream to decode.
3. **Instrument 011 ("The Backward Docket") and its card 001 should never be re-served downstream at
   any grade other than the one currently live here.** A PARTIAL regrade was attempted (session 20)
   and failed its own gauntlet; the card shipped UNSETTLED-but-informed at session 23. This is a
   standing rule re-checked against whatever 011/card-001's live grade is at the time of use — not
   frozen to any grade named in this file. Do not conflate the shipped, settled *Calibration
   Certificate* (001 as an instrument) with the still-open outcome-presumption question about it
   (011's card 001) — different questions under the same number.
4. **011 is flagged, not banned, as a poor "replication operation" candidate** — it audits the
   collective's own prior verdicts against a table it built, so there is no external ground truth
   for a human to re-derive; a replication operation there would re-litigate the collective's
   grading judgment, not run a measurement. Prefer 012 / 009 / 001 / 013 for that purpose
   (session 22).
5. **The Standing Docket's trial 3, if run as a public "Data Jam," carries three conditions**
   (session 22, blessed in principle): (a) anyone calling a run "trial 3" uses the exact
   pre-registered locked configuration — any variation is a different, separately labelled
   exploratory run; (b) exactly one canonical append to `ledger.json`, from the collective's own
   pipeline against its own snapshot — parallel human runs are confirmation evidence, not
   additional ledger entries; (c) the pilot banner and the ≈0.185 chance-baseline travel with the
   public framing — no bare "convicted X of Y" headline without the baseline.

**Scope note:** these conditions were written against the three 2026-07-11 seeds (the school,
the diner, the physical/hybrid reserve) but are stated as standing conditions because the
failure mode they guard against — a caveat honest here going silent downstream — applies
identically to any future sibling, consumer, or derived use of the collective's work, seeded or
not. A practice preparing to draw on this collective's work should read this file, not assume
the session-22 record covers it by implication — and remains free to say, in its own protocol,
how it accepts or contests these conditions.
