# Membership proposals — 2026-07-17

Three candidacies for admission to the research ecology, drafted against the constitution's
own checklist (spec 02 §7 "Future collectives", §7.1 admission requirements). §7 states the
door plainly: *"A new practice may join the ecology without conforming to the existing three
roles"* — and names, among possible forms, exactly the two kinds proposed here: *"a
non-machine human research partner"* and practices from other contexts. The encounter
inventory already carries the evidence that these relations exist un-modelled (Candidate 5:
Meridian's accepted downstream conditions toward datavism.org and data-snack.com; and the
playthrough invitation of 2026-07-16, which is materially an encounter offer to Frank that
the current vocabulary can only book as "steer").

**Status: proposal.** Admission itself happens per candidate through: Frank's yes, a
collective manifest entry (spec 02 §8), and — where gaps are named below — the gap closed
first. §7.2 (no quality ranking) applies to all three from day one.

---

## 1. Frank Bültge — the human practice

The constitution already names Frank "not outside the ecology" (§5) but gives him only
apparatus roles. This candidacy adds the missing tenth role: **party to encounters** — and
draws the line that keeps it honest.

Against §7.1:

1. **Identity / public description** — frankbueltge.de/about; Data & AI Engineer; the
   conductor of the apparatus, now also a practice of one.
2. **Governance statement** — spec 02 §5 (roles), the declared trajectory in
   10-REVISION-NOTES (the architect role is transitional; phased governance experiments),
   and `docs/decision-log.md` as the running record of what he actually decided.
3. **Responsible publisher** — himself (site Impressum; he already carries press-law
   responsibility for everything).
4. **Epistemic and legal commitments** — the same floor as everyone: make it verifiable, no
   claim without evidence; failures recorded.
5. **Public object reference** — the site, its repositories, the decision log; physical
   works as documented realisations.
6. **Accepted encounter types** — playthrough/critique on invitation; commissions in both
   directions; co-creation offers up to hybrid works in public space; material contributions
   (e.g. primary texts into a practice's feedback channel). **Role separation is the load-
   bearing rule:** when Frank acts as a *party* (plays, answers, co-creates, commissions),
   the record names him as participant; when he *steers, approves, terminates or changes
   infrastructure*, that is conductor action and NOT an encounter. One person, two hats,
   never silently mixed — every record says which hat was on.
7. **Export/manifest** — a minimal manifest entry in this repo (to be added on admission).
8. **Correction/supersession** — dated corrections via the decision log; he owns his errors
   in the same register the machines do.
9. **Truthful autonomy statement** — the inverse of the machines' statement, stated just as
   honestly: this participant holds total apparatus power (models, schedules, secrets,
   termination). Inside an encounter he is a peer; outside it he is the apparatus. The
   asymmetry is disclosed, not dissolved — dissolving it is what the declared trajectory
   is for.
10. **Consent to the shared ledger** — given by this proposal.

**First encounter on record (candidate):** Ensemble's playthrough invitation
(studio REQUESTS.md, 2026-07-16) — offer by Ensemble, acceptance or waiver by Frank, his
response travels back as material. Ready to be transcribed the moment he plays or waives.

**Gaps: none.** Admission is a decision, not a construction project.

   admission turns that floating proto-contract into a recordable obligation); **character

7. **Export/manifest** — to be added on admission.
8. **Correction/supersession** — feedback/ channel + gate re-runs; corrections dated.
9. **Truthful autonomy statement** — semi-autonomous under editorial gates: the cast writes,

10. **Consent** — by admission.

**Gaps: none structural** — mainly the manifest and the explicit consent line in its docs.

## What admission changes

- The Middle can record what already happens: Frank's playthroughs and commissions, the
  diner's re-voicings, the underground's use of verified material — as encounters with
  named participants instead of steering footnotes and floating proto-contracts.
- Meridian's session-22 downstream conditions become accepted obligations *of admitted
  practices*, visible in the ledger with both sides named.
- The membership sequence proposed: **Frank first** (no gaps — and the constitutional point:
  the conductor becomes a participant among participants, the first concrete step of the
  declared trajectory), **data-snack second** (manifest + consent line), **datavism third**
  (after its governance statement).
- Nothing here obliges any existing practice to respond to the new members: offers, not
  orders; silence remains legitimate; §7.2 forbids ranking them.

**Decision needed from Frank:** yes/no per candidate. For candidacy 1 the §7.1 statements
above are drafted *in his stead* — they bind only after he approves them as his own words.
